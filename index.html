About Me

Hello! I'm Danish Ghumman, a dedicated and dynamic Computer Science major from the University of Pittsburgh, driven by a passion for problem-solving and a steadfast commitment to continuous learning.

My journey into the world of technology began in an unexpected way. Originally on a path toward physical therapy, I made the bold decision to switch to computer science, despite my initial lack of knowledge in the field. This leap into the unknown was not just a change of major—it was the start of a new chapter in my life that ignited a fervent curiosity and a tenacious resolve to delve deeper into the complexities of computing.

In the beginning, I faced numerous challenges, from grasping fundamental concepts to applying them in practical, team-based projects. However, these hurdles did not deter me; they fueled my ambition and taught me the importance of adaptability and perseverance. These qualities have become the cornerstones of my approach to both personal and professional challenges, allowing me to thrive in a field that is perpetually evolving.

As I continue to advance in my career, I am eager to take on new challenges, contribute to groundbreaking projects, and further my education. With a strong foundation in both the theoretical and practical aspects of computer science, combined with my adaptability and perseverance, I am ready to make a significant impact in the tech industry.

Thank you for visiting my portfolio site. I am excited to explore opportunities to collaborate and innovate together.

Personal Philosophy Statement

I remember my sophomore year being quite the struggle as I made a drastic change in my academic career, switching majors. I decided to leave physical therapy to pursue a computer science degree. This change had come due to many factors but one thing was for sure, I did not know anything about computers. And there I was, thrown into my first group project unsure of what to do and given three weeks to figure it out. However, with the confusion and uncertainty, a hunger for knowledge blossomed and a passion for problem-solving that I hadn't realized I possessed. This experience taught me the value of adaptability and perseverance, traits that have since defined my approach to both work and learning.

Adaptability has become my cornerstone, allowing me to navigate the constantly evolving field of computer science with an open mind and a readiness to learn. In a domain where technologies and programming languages are perpetually everchanging, my ability to adapt quickly has been invaluable. Transitioning from theoretical coursework to practical, hands-on development required not only technical skill but also the ability to adapt to new tools and methodologies. My success in the previous experiences I’ve had was not just in the completion of the task but in the journey of learning and adaptation that accompanied it.

Perseverance, on the other hand, has empowered me to tackle challenges with determination and resilience. The journey from knowing nothing about computers to becoming proficient in languages like Java and Python was full of obstacles. Yet, it was my unwavering commitment to my goals that saw me through. My project on developing a Crossword Solver in Java is a testament to this perseverance. The project demanded not only a deep understanding of the language but also the creativity to devise a recursive backtracking algorithm—a challenge that I met with relentless effort and dedication.

As I look forward to embarking on new challenges, whether it be pursuing a challenging role in the tech industry, contributing to innovative projects, or furthering my education, I am confident that my adaptability and perseverance will be my greatest assets. These qualities have prepared me to tackle the unknown, to learn and grow from every experience, and to contribute meaningfully to my future endeavors and to the success of the organizations I will be a part of. My journey from a struggling sophomore to a proficient computer scientist is a testament to the transformative power of adaptability and perseverance, and it is these qualities that I bring to every project, team, and challenge I encounter.

My journey into the field of computer science at the University of Pittsburgh has not only been an academic pursuit but also a personal mission to bridge cultural gaps and foster an environment of inclusion and respect.

Internships

Junior Salesforce Administrator

My role involved utilizing SOQL (Salesforce Object Query Language) extensively to effectively manage and eliminate duplicate records in our database, significantly enhancing both data accuracy and integrity. This crucial task involved crafting precise queries to identify overlaps and redundancies, allowing for meticulous data cleanup and maintenance. Additionally, I implemented robust agile development methodologies within Salesforce, focusing on tiered user authentication mechanisms in test environments. This approach not only bolstered data security but also ensured finely tuned access control, preventing unauthorized data access and maintaining the confidentiality and integrity of sensitive information. These improvements were pivotal in streamlining data management processes and enhancing overall system security within the organization.

AI/Machine Learning Intern

During my internship at the University of Pittsburgh, I aided in the development of an innovative AI model using Python. This model is designed to accurately discern voice gender and age, facilitating real-time interactive feedback between parents and children, which can be particularly beneficial in educational and developmental applications. In addition to this, I significantly enhanced our audio analysis capabilities by developing a sophisticated algorithm utilizing both PyTorch and TensorFlow. This algorithm optimizes the voice data labeling process, streamlining the analysis of audio segments and improving the efficiency and accuracy of our data processing workflows.
